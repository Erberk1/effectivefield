A scientific treatise on the understanding of character and consciousness.

What makes you *you*? Is it the arrangement of atoms in your brain? The electrochemical signals racing along neurons? The memories accumulated over a lifetime?

Consciousness remains one of the deepest puzzles at the intersection of physics, neuroscience, and philosophy. This essay explores what science can — and cannot — tell us about the origins of character.
