Group theory is the mathematical language of symmetry — and symmetry is the deepest organizing principle in physics.

From Noether's theorem linking symmetries to conservation laws, to the gauge groups of the Standard Model, to the representation theory underlying quantum mechanics — groups are everywhere in modern physics.

This post explores the fundamental connection between abstract algebra and the physical world.
