This is a creative narrative reimagining the moment Charles Darwin, in his Victorian study, receives Alfred Russel Wallace's letter containing similar evolutionary conclusions.

A rebellious light beam entering through curtain gaps becomes the central metaphor — for Darwin's internal conflict between safety and revolutionary ideas, between the familiar darkness of silence and the blinding clarity of truth.

The curtain is comfort. The light is knowledge. Darwin must choose whether to let it in.
